The Ministry of Health announced on Sunday [3 Jul 2022] the detection of 21 new confirmed cases of cholera.

The ministry spokesman Saif Al-Badr said that "we have recorded 21 confirmed cases of cholera, including 10 new cases in Kirkuk, 7 in Dhi Qar, 2 in Al-Rusafa side of Baghdad, and 1 in each of Wasit and Al-Karkh side of Baghdad."

Al-Badr added, "these new infections are added to the cases we previously announced, bringing the total to 160 confirmed cases of cholera, and 2 deaths, the 1st in Kirkuk and the 2nd in Baghdad. The 160 recorded cases include 80 in Kirkuk, 37 in Al-Rusafa side of Baghdad, 13 in Dhi Qar, 10 in each of Sulaymaniyah and Diyala, 3 in Al-Muthanna, 2 in each of Babil, Al-Karkh side of Baghdad, and Wasit, and 1 in Al-Najaf.

[Cholera is an acute diarrheal infection caused by ingestion of food or water contaminated with the bacterium _Vibrio cholerae_. The report above shows that the number of cholera cases during the current outbreak in Iraq has increased to 160, with the 21 newly confirmed cases. Two deaths due to the disease have been recorded during the current outbreak.

Ensuring the safety of drinking water and food, hygiene awareness, enforcing monitoring measures, and strengthening disease surveillance are the primary measures to prevent and control cholera outbreaks. - Mod.NS
